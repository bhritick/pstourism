Day 1:

Arrival in Gangtok

Arrive at New Jalpaiguri (NJP) or Siliguri and take a shared cab, bus, or PST Cab service to Gangtok. Check in at the PST-designated hotel, where you'll enjoy a hot lunch of rice, dal, vegetables, and chicken/egg curry with fried potatoes.

Explore MG Marg

After a short rest, head out to explore MG Marg, the bustling heart of Gangtok, filled with shops, cafes, and vibrant local life.

Dinner and Bonfire (Optional)

Return to the hotel for a dinner of fried rice and chili chicken. You can also opt for a bonfire (extra cost) to round off the evening in a cozy atmosphere.

Day 2:

Journey to North Sikkim

Start your day early with a scenic drive to North Sikkim in a shared vehicle. Along the way, enjoy sightseeing as you head towards Lachung, a picturesque mountain village.

En Route Lunch and Arrival

Stop for lunch on the way before continuing to your hotel in Lachung. Upon arrival, check in, rest, and enjoy a warm dinner.

Lachung: Beauty and Chill

Lachung is known for its stunning beauty, but the weather can be quite cold, so make sure to dress warmly.

Day 3:

Exploring North Sikkim

After breakfast, embark on a tour of North Sikkim, visiting highlights like Zero Point and the breathtaking Yumthang Valley. Due to the high altitude, avoid strenuous activities to stay comfortable.

Return to Gangtok

Head back to the hotel for lunch, then begin your return journey to Gangtok. Settle in for an overnight stay in Gangtok to relax after a day of exploration.

Day 4:

Departure Day

On the final day, enjoy a morning stroll around the nearby areas, followed by breakfast. Afterward, check out and head to your next destination.

Customize Your Trip

You can further personalize your trip to include popular spots like Changu Lake, Baba Mandir, Nathula Pass (India-China border), Gangtok local sightseeing, Pelling, or Ravangla.

FAQs :

Is it safe to travel to North Sikkim with children?
Many families travel to North Sikkim with children as young as 2 years old, and even elderly travelers in their 70s visit. While there is a lack of oxygen and medical facilities, with proper care and precautions, it is generally safe. It's best to consult your doctor before traveling.
