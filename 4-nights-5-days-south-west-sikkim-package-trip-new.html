Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival at NJP & Transfer to Pelling

  • Arrival: At NJP Railway Station or Bagdogra Airport.
  • Drive: Begin your journey to Pelling (approx. 6-7 hours drive).
  • Check-in: At your Pelling accommodation and relax after the drive.
  • Overnight Stay: Pelling.

Day 2: Pelling Sightseeing

  • After Breakfast: Explore the best of Pelling:
  • Pelling Glass Skywalk: Asia’s highest glass skywalk with panoramic views of Mt. Kanchenjunga and surrounding peaks.
  • Sangachoeling Monastery: A peaceful and historic monastery offering serene views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Kanchenjunga Waterfalls: One of the most beautiful waterfalls in the region.
  • Rimbi Waterfalls: A tranquil waterfall located amidst lush green forests.
  • Khecheopalri Lake: A sacred lake surrounded by dense forests, known for its tranquil atmosphere.
  • Return: To your hotel for a relaxed evening.
  • Overnight Stay: Pelling.

Day 3: Pelling – Ravangla & Bhaleydunga Ropeway

  • After Breakfast: Head towards Ravangla (approx. 2-3 hours drive).
  • Check-in: At your Ravangla accommodation.
  • Visit: The famous Buddha Park in Ravangla, home to a large statue of Buddha and beautiful surroundings.
  • Experience: The Bhaleydunga Ropeway for a thrilling ride with scenic views of the valley and surrounding peaks.
  • Explore: The nearby Ralang Monastery, known for its impressive architecture and peaceful ambiance.
  • Overnight Stay: Ravangla.

Day 4: Ravangla – Namchi Chardham & Temi Tea Garden

  • After Breakfast: Drive to Namchi (approx. 1 hour).
  • Visit: Namchi Chardham, a unique pilgrimage site with replicas of the four main Char Dhams of India.
  • Explore: Temi Tea Garden, known for its scenic tea plantations and panoramic views of the Kanchenjunga range.
  • Return: To Ravangla and relax for the evening.
  • Overnight Stay: Ravangla.

Day 5: Departure from Ravangla to NJP

  • After Breakfast: Check out and drive back to NJP Railway Station or Bagdogra Airport (approx. 5-6 hours).
  • Depart: With wonderful memories of South West Sikkim’s serene landscapes and spiritual experiences.
