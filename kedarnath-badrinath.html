Overview

Kedarnath and Badrinath, two iconic pilgrimage destinations nestled in the Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, are revered by millions of Hindus worldwide for their profound spiritual significance and breathtaking natural beauty.

Kedarnath, perched at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet), is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Legend has it that the temple was built by the Pandavas, heroes of the Indian epic Mahabharata. Surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks and the Mandakini River, Kedarnath offers a serene ambiance for spiritual seekers.

Badrinath, at an elevation of 3,133 meters (10,279 feet), is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple stands amidst the picturesque landscape of the Himalayas, with the Alaknanda River nearby. Pilgrims believe a visit to Badrinath purifies the soul.

Day Wise Itinerary

Day 1: Haridwar to Guptkashi (220 kms) | Temple Visit

Get picked up from Haridwar and transferred to Guptkashi. Visit Ardh Narishwar Temple and retire for the night at the hotel.

Day 2: Guptkashi - Gauri Kund - Kedarnath

Early morning trek from Gauri Kund to Kedarnath (20 km). Darshan at Kedarnath Temple. Overnight stay in Kedarnath.

Day 3: Kedarnath to Guptkashi

Trek back to Guptkashi and visit Gauri Kund Temple.

Day 4: Guptkashi to Pipalkoti (210 Kms)

Drive to Pipalkoti via Tungnath temple. Overnight stay in Pipalkoti.

Day 5: Pipalkoti - Badrinath - Pipalkoti

Visit Badrinath, Mana Village, and Narsingh Temple in Joshimath. Overnight stay in Pipalkoti.

Day 6: Pipalkoti - Rishikesh - Haridwar

Drive back to Haridwar via Rishikesh. End the tour with sweet memories.
