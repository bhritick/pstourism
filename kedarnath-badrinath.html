Overview

Kedarnath and Badrinath, two iconic pilgrimage destinations nestled in the Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, are revered by millions of Hindus worldwide for their profound spiritual significance and breathtaking natural beauty.

Kedarnath, perched at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level, is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Legend has it that the temple was initially built by the Pandavas, the heroes of the Indian epic Mahabharata. Surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks and the gushing Mandakini River, Kedarnath offers a serene ambiance for devotees seeking divine blessings and spiritual solace.

Badrinath, situated at an elevation of 3,133 meters (10,279 feet), is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his form as Badrinath. Adorned with vibrant colors and intricate carvings, the temple stands amidst the picturesque landscape of the Himalayas, with the Alaknanda River flowing nearby. Pilgrims believe that a visit to Badrinath purifies the soul and bestows divine grace.

Day Wise Tour Itinerary

Day 1: Haridwar to Guptkashi (220 kms)

Get picked up from Haridwar/Rishikesh and transfer to Guptkashi. Visit Ardh Narishwar Temple and retire for the day.

Day 2: Guptkashi - Kedarnath

Leave early around 5 AM for Kedarnath Temple. A 20 km hike or pony ride will take you to the serene Kedarnath Temple.

Day 3: Kedarnath to Guptkashi

Trek back to Guptkashi with a stop at Gauri Kund. Overnight stay at Guptkashi.

Day 4: Guptkashi to Pipalkoti

After breakfast, drive to Pipalkoti via Tungnath. Enjoy the scenic beauty and check-in for the night.

Day 5: Pipalkoti - Badrinath

Journey to Badrinath Temple with visits to Narsingh Temple and Mana Village. Return to Pipalkoti for the night.

Day 6: Pipalkoti - Haridwar

After breakfast, head to Haridwar via Rishikesh. Trip ends with happy memories!
